What is ELV?

Understanding Extra Low Voltage systems and why they're essential for modern Dubai buildings

The Foundation of Smart Buildings

ELV systems operate at voltages below 50V AC (75V DC), making them safe, efficient, and ideal for low-power building technologies. They carry signals and power for all the systems that keep modern buildings intelligent, secure, and compliant.

In Dubai's rapid development, ELV infrastructure is non-negotiable. Whether it's a new commercial tower, a luxury villa, or a mixed-use development, every building needs a robust ELV backbone.

  • Safe to install and maintain
  • Supports integrated building management
  • Enables security and life safety systems
  • Scalable and future-proof

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ELV systems in Dubai

What's the difference between ELV and power wiring?

▼
ELV (Extra Low Voltage) systems operate at voltages below 50V AC or 75V DC, carrying signals and control power for security, communication, and automation systems. Power wiring operates at 230V or higher and delivers electricity to lights, appliances, and HVAC systems. While power systems use thicker cables and larger conduits, ELV systems focus on data integrity and signal clarity. Both must be properly segregated and installed to separate standards.

How long does an ELV installation typically take?

▼
Timeline depends heavily on project scope and building type. A small villa fit-out might take 2-4 weeks, while a full commercial tower ELV installation can span 3-6 months. Our team works closely with general contractors to coordinate with other trades, minimize delays, and ensure quality testing before handover. We provide detailed project schedules during the planning phase.

Can ELV systems be integrated with existing BMS platforms?

▼
Absolutely. One of ELV's key advantages is interoperability. Modern ELV systems use standard protocols like BACnet, Modbus, and MQTT to communicate with building management systems. Whether you're retrofitting into an older BMS or integrating into a new system, our engineers design ELV architecture to connect seamlessly. We handle gateway configuration, network integration, and thorough testing.

What happens to ELV systems during power outages?

▼
ELV systems themselves consume minimal power, but they do require a power source. We design systems with UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) backup for critical functions like fire alarms, emergency lighting control, access control egress, and security monitoring. The UPS ensures these life-safety systems remain operational during outages. Battery capacity is sized based on backup duration requirements.

How do you ensure data security in ELV systems?

▼
Security starts with proper physical segregation—ELV cabling is kept separate from power and RF sources to prevent interference and eavesdropping. For networked ELV systems, we implement firewalls, encryption, and access controls. Regular security audits and firmware updates are part of ongoing maintenance. We follow UAE cyber security guidelines and SIRA standards to ensure your building's systems are protected against unauthorized access.
